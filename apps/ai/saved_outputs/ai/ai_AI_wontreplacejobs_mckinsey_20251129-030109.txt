### ONE SENTENCE SUMMARY
Artificial intelligence will transform the economy by creating partnerships between humans and machines, augmenting jobs through redesigned workflows rather than causing mass unemployment.

### SUMMARY
A McKinsey Global Institute report argues AI will augment, not replace, most jobs, requiring humans to guide AI and organizations to redesign work itself.

### IDEAS
1.  Current technologies possess the technical potential to automate approximately 57% of all U.S. work hours.
2.  The future of work is envisioned as a partnership among people, AI agents, and robots.
3.  Capturing AI's potential economic value, estimated at $2.9 trillion in the U.S. by 2030, is entirely dependent on human guidance.
4.  Over 70% of skills currently sought by employers are utilized in both automatable and non-automatable work.
5.  Highly specialized cognitive skills, like routine accounting and specific programming languages, are predicted to face the most significant disruption.
6.  Workers will need to shift their focus from performing tasks to framing questions and interpreting AI-generated results.
7.  Skills rooted in social and emotional intelligence, such as conflict resolution and design thinking, will remain uniquely human.
8.  Job roles centered on assisting and caring for others are likely to experience the least amount of change.
9.  Realizing economic gains from AI requires redesigning entire workflows, not just automating individual tasks.
10. Humans will remain essential for oversight, quality control, and providing a presence preferred by customers and patients.
11. Demand for AI fluency has grown sevenfold in just two years, marking it as the fastest-growing skill in U.S. job postings.
12. The economic adjustment is rapidly moving toward new forms of human-AI collaboration.
13. Historical precedent suggests that employment is more likely to evolve rather than contract in response to major technological shifts.
14. Human intelligence is shifting from a focus on execution to one of orchestration and judgment.
15. AI's role is compared to the calculator, which freed mathematicians to solve higher-level problems.
16. The integration of AI is described as a "reimagining of work itself," not a simple technology rollout.
17. Success hinges on redesigning processes, roles, skills, culture, and metrics to foster synergy between people, agents, and robots.
18. The high potential for task automation does not directly translate to an inevitable loss of jobs.
19. Empathy, creativity, and contextual understanding are human attributes that are difficult for machines to replicate.
20. The report was authored by a team of seven researchers from the McKinsey Global Institute.
